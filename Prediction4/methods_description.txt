----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Data retrieval
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Programmatic access via BrAPI v1 on T3/Wheat:

  • GET  /brapi/v1/studies/{studyId}/germplasm
       — focal trial accession list (germplasmName) for each prediction trial.

  • GET  /brapi/v1/studies/{studyId}
       — focal trial latitude, longitude, startDate, endDate (for weather).

Local inputs (per challenge-provided study bundles):

  • Training phenotypes: CSV files under unzipped/study{study_id}/,
    filtered to trait "Grain yield - kg/ha|CO_321:0001218".

  • Training trial metadata: training_trial_metadata_*.csv (locations, dates).

  • Focal genotypes: VCF files under "Genotype Data for Prediction Trials/"
    (competition download).

  • Supplementary training VCFs: URLs from training_vcf_names_*.csv; files are
    cached under training_vcf_cache/ and merged when they share sufficient
    markers with the focal VCF (same SNP set).

Public weather: Open-Meteo Historical API (archive-api.open-meteo.com) for
daily temperature and precipitation over each trial’s growing window, with
JSON caching under weather_cache/.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
2. One-step vs two-step model
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Two-step:

  Step 1 — Phenotype layer (BLUEs):
    For each CV scenario, training observations exclude the focal trial;
    CV00 additionally excludes any observation on focal-trial accessions.
    Trial effects are removed (adjusted_ij = y_ij − trial_mean_j + grand_mean).
    Per-accession targets are aggregated as weighted means of adjusted values:
    training trials that are environmentally more similar to the focal trial
    (see Section 4-B) receive higher weight (G×E-informed BLUEs).

  Step 2 — Genomic prediction:
    GRM-based GBLUP on the focal (+ merged) VCF samples using all retained SNPs
    after QC (see Section 5).


----------------------------------------------------------------------
3. Cross-validation scenarios (CV0 / CV00)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Implemented exactly as in Jarquin et al. (2017) / challenge specification:

  • CV0: the focal trial is excluded from training; other trials may contribute,
    including phenotypes on lines that also appear in the focal trial.

  • CV00: the focal trial is excluded, and all phenotypes on focal-trial
    accessions in other trials are excluded before fitting.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Environmental data and G×E
----------------------------------------------------------------------

4-A  Weather features (per trial location and date window)

  From Open-Meteo daily series: temperature_2m_mean/max/min, precipitation_sum.
  Derived scalar features include: temp_mean, temp_std, growing degree days
  (GDD, base 0 °C), heat_days (T_max > 32 °C), frost_days (T_min < −2 °C),
  precip_total, precip_days; elevation is taken from training metadata when
  present.

4-B  G×E weighting (no uniform yield shift)

  A vector of weather features is built for the focal trial and for each
  training trial (when coordinates and dates allow). Features are scaled and
  Euclidean distances to the focal trial are converted to Gaussian similarity
  weights w ∈ (0, 1]. These weights are applied when aggregating adjusted
  phenotypes to accession-level BLUEs (Step 1).

  Note: We do NOT apply a single additive “environmental correction” to all
  predicted yields. Such a constant shift does not change Pearson correlation
  between predictions and observed values; the environmental signal is
  incorporated through weighted BLUEs instead.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
5. Genotype QC and GRM-based GBLUP
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Genotypes are read from VCF (biallelic SNPs; GT 0/1, unphased or phased).

  • Markers are filtered by minor allele frequency (MAF > 0.01) and missing
    call rate per marker (< 0.5); remaining missing values are mean-imputed.

  • Genotypes are column-centred; the genomic relationship matrix is
        G = X X' / p
    where p is the number of SNPs after QC (no random marker subsampling).

  • GBLUP: predict breeding values by solving the mixed-model equivalent on the
    training set with genomic relationship G_tt among training individuals,
    ridge term λ I, and BLUP prediction to non-training rows via G_all,train.

  • λ is selected by leave-one-out cross-validation on the training set
    (grid search on log-spaced λ).

  • Small ridge on the diagonal of G (1e-4) is added for numerical stability.

  • If fewer than three training individuals overlap the VCF, predictions fall
    back to the global mean of training BLUEs.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
6. Blending observed BLUE with genomic prediction
----------------------------------------------------------------------

For accessions present in the training set, final predictions blend genomic
GBLUP with the accession BLUE:

    prediction = w × BLUE + (1 − w) × GBLUP

Per-trial weights w (blend_obs) are chosen so that trials with small training
overlap rely more on BLUE (e.g. w ≈ 0.95 for very data-limited trials) and
trials with large training overlap rely more on the genomic signal (e.g.
w ≈ 0.50 for YT_Urb_25). Default w = 0.70 when a trial is not listed.
